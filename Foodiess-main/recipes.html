Spaghetti Carbonara

‚è±Ô∏è 30 mins
üçΩÔ∏è 4 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil and cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta or guanciale and cook until crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside, leaving the fat in the pan.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, and black pepper until well combined.
  4. When the pasta is done, reserve 1 cup of the pasta water, then drain the pasta.
  5. Working quickly, add the hot pasta to the skillet with the pancetta fat. Toss to coat the pasta in the fat.
  6. Remove the skillet from heat and immediately pour in the egg mixture, tossing constantly to coat the pasta. The residual heat will cook the eggs into a creamy sauce.
  7. If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it.
  8. Stir in the crispy pancetta and serve immediately with extra grated cheese and black pepper.

Chef's Tips

  • Use the highest quality eggs you can find since they're a main component of the sauce.
  • Traditional carbonara uses guanciale (cured pork cheek), but pancetta or even bacon can be substituted.
  • The key to perfect carbonara is timing - have all your ingredients prepped before you start cooking.
  • Never add cream to carbonara - the creaminess comes from the emulsified eggs and cheese.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g Pecorino Romano, grated
  • 50g Parmigiano Reggiano, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water

Nutrition Info

Per serving:

  • Calories: 650
  • Carbs: 75g
  • Protein: 30g
  • Fat: 25g

Beef Tacos

‚è±Ô∏è 30 mins
üçΩÔ∏è 4 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Easy

Instructions

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
  2. Drain excess fat from the beef, then return to the heat. Add the taco seasoning and water, stirring to combine. Simmer for 5 minutes until thickened.
  3. While the beef cooks, prepare your taco toppings: shred the lettuce, dice the tomatoes, slice the avocado, and crumble the cheese.
  4. Warm the taco shells according to package directions (usually in the oven or microwave).
  5. Assemble the tacos by filling each shell with the seasoned beef, then adding your desired toppings.
  6. Serve immediately with lime wedges and your favorite hot sauce.

Chef's Tips

  • For extra flavor, toast your taco shells in a dry skillet for 30 seconds per side.
  • Make it vegetarian by substituting the beef with black beans or lentils.
  • For a healthier option, use lettuce leaves instead of taco shells.
  • The beef mixture can be made ahead and reheated when ready to serve.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (85% lean)
  • 1 packet taco seasoning (or homemade blend)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 12 taco shells (hard or soft)
  • 2 cups shredded lettuce
  • 1 large tomato, diced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup salsa
  • Lime wedges for serving

Nutrition Info

Per serving (3 tacos):

  • Calories: 520
  • Carbs: 35g
  • Protein: 28g
  • Fat: 30g

Fluffy Pancakes

‚è±Ô∏è 20 mins
üçΩÔ∏è 4 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Easy

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, beat the egg, then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined (a few lumps are okay).
  4. Heat a griddle or large skillet over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake onto the griddle.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2-3 minutes.
  7. Flip and cook until golden brown on the other side, about 1-2 minutes more.
  8. Serve warm with maple syrup and butter.

Chef's Tips

  • Don't overmix the batter - this makes pancakes tough.
  • Let the batter rest for 5-10 minutes for fluffier pancakes.
  • For blueberry pancakes, add fresh blueberries to the batter after pouring onto the griddle.
  • Keep cooked pancakes warm in a 200¬∞F oven while cooking the rest.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Maple syrup and butter for serving

Nutrition Info

Per serving (3 pancakes):

  • Calories: 320
  • Carbs: 45g
  • Protein: 8g
  • Fat: 12g

Avocado Toast

üçΩÔ∏è 2 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Easy

Instructions

  1. Toast the bread until golden and crisp.
  2. Cut the avocado in half, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl.
  3. Mash the avocado with a fork to your desired consistency (chunky or smooth).
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  5. Spread the mashed avocado evenly on the toast.
  6. Top with your choice of additional toppings (see suggestions below).
  7. Serve immediately.

Chef's Tips

  • Use ripe but firm avocados for best texture.
  • Try different bread types like sourdough, whole grain, or rye.
  • Popular toppings: poached eggs, cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, red pepper flakes, microgreens.
  • For extra flavor, drizzle with olive oil or balsamic glaze.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of bread (your choice)

  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, poached eggs, red pepper flakes

Classic Caesar Salad

‚è±Ô∏è 15 mins
üçΩÔ∏è 2 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. For the croutons: Preheat oven to 375¬∞F. Toss bread cubes with olive oil, garlic powder, and salt. Spread on a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes until golden and crisp.
  2. For the dressing: In a blender, combine garlic, anchovies, egg yolk, Dijon, Worcestershire, lemon juice, and Parmesan. Blend until smooth.
  3. With blender running, slowly drizzle in olive oil until emulsified. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. In a large bowl, toss romaine lettuce with dressing to coat (you may not need all the dressing).
  5. Add croutons and toss gently.
  6. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan and freshly ground black pepper.

Chef's Tips

  • Use day-old bread for best crouton texture.
  • For a vegetarian version, omit the anchovies.
  • Add grilled chicken or shrimp to make it a main course.
  • Dressing can be made ahead and stored in the fridge for 3 days.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 2 cups bread cubes (French or Italian bread)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

    • For the dressing:

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 anchovy fillets
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Spinach Quiche

‚è±Ô∏è 1 hour
üçΩÔ∏è 6 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375¬∞F. Roll out pie crust and fit into a 9-inch pie dish. Crimp edges and prick bottom with a fork. Line with parchment and pie weights or dried beans.
  2. Blind bake for 15 minutes. Remove weights and parchment and bake 5 more minutes until lightly golden. Let cool slightly.
  3. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook until soft, about 5 minutes. Add spinach and cook until wilted, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  5. Sprinkle half the cheese over the crust. Spread spinach mixture evenly over cheese. Pour egg mixture over top. Sprinkle with remaining cheese.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes until center is set and top is golden brown.
  7. Let cool for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

Chef's Tips

  • Use frozen spinach (thawed and squeezed dry) for convenience.
  • Add cooked bacon or mushrooms for extra flavor.
  • Quiche can be made ahead and reheated in a 300¬∞F oven for 15 minutes.
  • For a gluten-free version, use a potato crust or crustless.

Ingredients

  • 1 pie crust (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 10 oz fresh spinach, chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/8 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Swiss cheese

Nutrition Info

Per serving:

  • Calories: 380

Beef Stroganoff

‚è±Ô∏è 45 mins
üçΩÔ∏è 4 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. Season beef with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat 1 tbsp oil over high heat. Sear beef in batches until browned, about 2 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
  2. In same skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add mushrooms and onions, cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant. Sprinkle flour over vegetables and stir to coat.
  4. Slowly whisk in beef broth, then Worcestershire sauce and Dijon mustard. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Return beef and any juices to the skillet. Reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 20 minutes until beef is tender.
  6. Stir in sour cream and heat through (do not boil). Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve over egg noodles or mashed potatoes, garnished with parsley.

Chef's Tips

  • Use tender cuts like sirloin or tenderloin for best results.
  • For extra richness, stir in 2 tbsp cream cheese with the sour cream.
  • Make ahead tip: Prepare through step 5, then refrigerate. Reheat and add sour cream just before serving.
  • For a lighter version, use Greek yogurt instead of sour cream.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • Chopped parsley for garnish
  • Egg noodles or mashed potatoes for serving

Nutrition Info

Per serving (without noodles):

  • Calories: 480
  • Carbs: 12g
  • Protein: 38g

Kung Pao Chicken

‚è±Ô∏è 25 mins
üçΩÔ∏è 4 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine chicken with cornstarch, soy sauce, and rice wine. Let marinate for 10 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, make the sauce: whisk together soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and cornstarch in a small bowl.
  3. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add chicken and stir-fry until no longer pink, about 3-4 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add a bit more oil if needed, then stir-fry garlic, ginger, and dried chilies for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add bell peppers and stir-fry for 2 minutes until slightly softened.
  6. Return chicken to the wok. Stir the sauce mixture and pour over chicken. Cook until sauce thickens, about 1-2 minutes.
  7. Stir in peanuts and green onions. Remove from heat.
  8. Serve hot over steamed rice.

Chef's Tips

  • Adjust the number of dried chilies to control the heat level.
  • For extra crunch, add vegetables like zucchini or water chestnuts.
  • Use cashews instead of peanuts for a variation.
  • Make it vegetarian by substituting chicken with tofu or mushrooms.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs boneless chicken thighs, cubed
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice wine or sherry
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 8-10 dried red chilies
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 3 green onions, sliced

    • For the sauce:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp water
  • Steamed rice for serving

Nutrition Info

Per serving (without rice):

  • Calories: 380
  • Carbs: 15g
  • Protein: 35g

Pork Dumplings

‚è±Ô∏è 1 hour
üçΩÔ∏è 6 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine pork, cabbage, green onions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, and egg. Mix well.
  2. Place a dumpling wrapper on a clean surface. Spoon about 1 tablespoon of filling into the center.
  3. Moisten the edges with water. Fold in half and pleat the edges to seal. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling.
  4. To pan-fry: Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add dumplings in a single layer and cook until bottoms are golden, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add 1/4 cup water to the skillet, cover immediately, and steam for 5-6 minutes until water evaporates.
  6. Remove lid and cook another minute until bottoms are crisp.
  7. Serve hot with dipping sauce.

Chef's Tips

  • Make extra and freeze uncooked dumplings on a baking sheet before transferring to a freezer bag.
  • For a shortcut, use store-bought wonton wrappers.
  • Try different fillings like shrimp, chicken, or vegetarian options.
  • For steamed dumplings, place in a steamer basket lined with cabbage leaves and steam for 8-10 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 2 cups finely chopped cabbage
  • 3 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 egg
  • 1 package round dumpling wrappers (about 50)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil for frying

    • For dipping sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp chili oil (optional)
  • 1 tsp sugar

Nutrition Info

Per serving (about 8 dumplings):

  • Calories: 320

‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375¬∞F. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook until soft, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant. Stir in chicken, cumin, chili powder, and salt. Cook 2 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in 1 cup cheese and cilantro.
  4. Spread 1/2 cup enchilada sauce in bottom of a 9x13 baking dish.
  5. Warm tortillas briefly to make pliable. Spoon about 1/3 cup chicken mixture down center of each tortilla. Roll up and place seam-side down in baking dish.

  8. Let stand 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with toppings as desired.

  • Use rotisserie chicken for a shortcut.
  • For extra flavor, add black beans or corn to the filling.

  • For spicier enchiladas, use hot enchilada sauce or add diced jalape√±os to filling.

  • 2 cups shredded Monterey Jack or cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro

Mushroom Risotto

‚è±Ô∏è 40 mins
üçΩÔ∏è 4 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. Heat broth in a saucepan and keep warm over low heat.
  2. In a large skillet, heat 2 tbsp butter and olive oil over medium heat. Add mushrooms and cook until golden, about 5 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  3. In same skillet, add remaining butter and onion. Cook until soft, about 3 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add rice and stir to coat with butter. Cook 1-2 minutes until rice is translucent around edges.
  5. Add wine and cook, stirring, until absorbed.
  6. Add warm broth 1/2 cup at a time, stirring frequently and allowing liquid to absorb before adding more.
  7. After about 18 minutes, when rice is al dente and creamy, stir in mushrooms, Parmesan, and parsley.
  8. Season with salt and pepper. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan.

Chef's Tips

  • Use Arborio or Carnaroli rice for best results.
  • For extra flavor, use a mix of wild mushrooms.
  • Stir constantly for creamier risotto.
  • Add a splash of cream at the end for extra richness.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 4 tbsp butter, divided
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lb mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, or wild), sliced
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Nutrition Info

Per serving:

  • Calories: 450
  • Carbs: 55g
  • Protein: 12g
  • Fat: 18g

Tiramisu

‚è±Ô∏è 30 mins + chilling
üçΩÔ∏è 8 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Medium

Instructions

  1. Brew coffee and let cool to room temperature. Stir in coffee liqueur if using.
  2. In a large bowl, beat egg yolks and sugar with an electric mixer until thick and pale, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add mascarpone and beat until smooth.
  4. In another bowl, beat cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold into mascarpone mixture.
  5. Quickly dip ladyfingers one at a time into coffee mixture (don't soak them) and arrange in a single layer in an 8x8 dish.
  6. Spread half the mascarpone cream over ladyfingers. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and remaining cream.
  7. Cover and refrigerate at least 6 hours, preferably overnight.
  8. Before serving, dust with cocoa powder.

Chef's Tips

  • For alcohol-free version, omit liqueur and use strong coffee.
  • Use fresh, high-quality mascarpone for best results.
  • For extra decadence, add a layer of chocolate shavings between the layers.
  • Make individual servings in glasses for an elegant presentation.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 2 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 16 oz mascarpone cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 24 ladyfinger cookies
  • Cocoa powder for dusting

Nutrition Info

Per serving:

  • Calories: 420
  • Carbs: 28g
  • Protein: 7g
  • Fat: 32g

Grilled Salmon

‚≠ê Difficulty: Easy

Instructions

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat (about 400¬∞F).

üçΩÔ∏è 4 servings
‚≠ê Difficulty: Easy

Instructions

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions. Drain, reserving 1/2 cup pasta water.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil and 2 tbsp butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add shrimp and cook 1-2 minutes per side until pink. Remove and set aside.
  4. In same skillet, add remaining butter, garlic, and red pepper flakes. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Add wine and lemon juice. Simmer 2 minutes.
  6. Return shrimp to skillet along with cooked pasta. Toss to combine, adding pasta water as needed to create a light sauce.
  7. Stir in parsley and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve immediately with lemon wedges and extra Parmesan if desired.

Chef's Tips

  • Use large or jumbo shrimp for best results.
  • For extra richness, stir in 1/4 cup heavy cream at the end.
  • Make it gluten-free by using gluten-free pasta.
  • Add vegetables like spinach or cherry tomatoes with the garlic for extra nutrition.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz linguine or spaghetti
  • 1 1/2 lbs large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 tbsp butter, divided
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Grated Parmesan (optional)
